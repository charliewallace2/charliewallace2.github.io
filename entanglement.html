Quantum Entanglement Interactive Comic

January 2025 - May 2025


Description:

In this group project, we were asked to incorporate a quantum mechanic into an interactive comic-style game. Our group got quantum entanglement, so we researched various studies, experiments, and theories related to QE, compiling everything on a whiteboard to help us select the most relevant concepts for the comic. We ultimately decided we wanted to include “spooky action at a distance,” the double-slit experiment, and Von Neumann entropy somewhere in the comic. We wanted the comic to show how quantum entanglement challenges how we usually think about cause and effect, and how it forces us to deal with uncertainty and missing information. We picked each concept to help get across those complicated ideas. The puzzle I worked on was the Lab puzzle based on entropy, which centers around an “Entropy Meter” at the top of the screen, which increases over time. As the meter rises, the TV screens begin to go black, symbolizing the loss of information; the player must then remember which shape was on each screen to make a match—unless the match was already found.
